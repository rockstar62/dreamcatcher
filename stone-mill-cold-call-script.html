The Stone Mill Cold Call Script
Introductory · Awareness · Seed Planting
Your Opener
Deliver this — then stop and listen
"Good morning, this is Steve with The Stone Mill. I'm reaching out to spread the word that your stone doesn't have to come from six hundred miles away — we produce it right here in Michigan. I could really use your help — would you be opposed to me sending a brochure over to you right now while I have you on the phone?"
Chris Voss: "Would you be opposed" invites a no — which makes them feel safe and opens them up.
🎯
Goal of this call: Awareness and introduction only. Get the email. Send the brochure live. Make her the hero who found the local source. Do not sell stone.
When They Respond
You say
"Perfect — what's the best email to send it to?"

[Get the email. Send it immediately.]

"I just sent it — do you mind pulling it up real quick? Just want to make sure it came through."
Then
Walk her through it briefly while she has it open. Ask who else on the team would want to see it. She's now the person who found the local Michigan stone source.
You say
"A local source?"

[Pause. Let it land.]

"I'm not asking you to change anything — just want to make sure you know we're here. Would you be opposed to keeping our brochure on file, just in case?"
Then
If she softens, get the email and send it. If she holds firm, thank her by name, log the contact, move on. The seed is planted.
You say
"Natural quarried stone — we own the quarries right here in Michigan. Everything from fieldstone to ledge to boulders. It's all in the brochure — would you be opposed to me sending it over right now?"
Then
Curiosity is an open door. This is your best outcome. Get the email and send it live.
You say
"I appreciate that — before you do, would you be opposed to grabbing a copy of our brochure for yourself? I'd love for you to have it too."
Then
Get her email before the transfer. If you reach the designer, deliver the opener fresh. Two seeds planted on one call.
You say
"That's really helpful, I appreciate it. Would you be opposed to me sending a brochure directly to you so you have something to pass along? Makes it easy."
Then
You're making her job easier. She's likely to say yes. Get the email and send it — now she's your internal advocate.
When She Gives You The Email
1
Send the brochure immediately — while she's still on the phone. Not later.
2
"I just sent it" — ask her to pull it up to confirm it came through.
3
Walk her through it briefly — one or two things to notice. Keep it short.
4
Log in HubSpot immediately after the call — name, email, outcome, next step.
