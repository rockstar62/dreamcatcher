You have a business doing $1.7M with strong margins, 4th-generation expertise, real stone quarries, and a clear exit vision. The One Constraint

Critical — Right Now

Customer Concentration = Your #1 Existential Risk

One reseller. ~70% of revenue. No contract. A handshake. If that relationship ends tomorrow, you go from $1.7M to roughly $500K overnight. Every other goal in this document is secondary to reducing this single point of failure. The 5-year exit only works if the business is sellable — and no PE firm buys a business with this exposure. This gets tracked visibly, weekly, no exceptions.

Phase 1 — Validation

1
Warm Contact Validation
Weeks 1–3: Now through July 11

Call the builders and architects you already know. Not cold. Warm. Tell them you're taking Stone Mill direct-to-builder and you want their honest reaction to your pricing. This is Step 2 of the Dalio Loop — diagnose whether the premium B2B hypothesis is real before scaling the machine to 20 touches/week. Three positive conversations = green light to accelerate. One hard no = free intel on why not. Do not skip this phase to go cold calling first.

  • Identify 5–8 warm contacts in builder/architect segment
  • Call them using your builder-to-builder language (not the script — your words)
  • Ask: "Would you rather buy stone directly from a Michigan quarry at my price, or keep going through a distributor?" — that's the Voss framing
  • Log every response in HubSpot
📞

Phase 2 — Cold Ramp

2
Prospecting Engine Ignition
Weeks 4–10: July 13 – August 28

Warm contacts validated the model. Now you scale. Use the Cold Call Playbook. Use the HubSpot pipeline. Use the brochure-send-on-call sequence. Weekly touch targets:

  • Week 4: 5 touches
  • Week 5–6: 10 touches/week
  • Week 7–8: 15 touches/week
  • Week 9–10: 20 touches/week — hold this rate

One call block each morning, 9–11am, Mon–Thu. Friday for follow-ups and admin. No exceptions for first 4 weeks to build the habit.

👤

Phase 3 — Hire & Hand Off

3
Sales Process Coordinator
Weeks 8–16: August – October 1

As volume grows you hit a wall — one person can't do 20+ outbound touches AND manage follow-up sequences AND keep HubSpot clean AND do quarry ops. The SPC role offloads everything except the conversation. Write the job description in Week 8. Post it. Interview in September. Target onboarding by October 1 so you have 4 weeks to train before Florida. This is the most important hire you'll make this year.

  • Write SPC job description (Week 8)
  • Post on Indeed and LinkedIn (Week 9)
  • Interview top 3 candidates (Week 11–12)
  • Hire and onboard (Week 13–14)
  • Transfer HubSpot, sequences, follow-up ownership (Week 15–16)

🌴 Florida Season — November to April
You can and should work from Florida. The machine you're building now is designed to run whether you're in Big Rapids or on the Gulf Coast. Here's how Florida mode looks different from Michigan mode — and what stays the same.
✅

Stays the Same in Florida

These Don't Stop

  • High-value builder conversations (you close deals on the phone)
  • Weekly check-in call with SPC (pipeline review, 30 min)
  • HubSpot pipeline oversight — review weekly, comment on flagged deals
  • Strategy planning and tool development (this is actually better in Florida — fewer interruptions)
  • Quarry land research (remote research, phone calls with sellers)
  • Monthly scorecard review with SPC
🔄

What Changes in Florida

SPC Picks Up the Operational Load

  • SPC owns all outbound prospecting — cold calls, sequence management, HubSpot entry
  • You shift from doing outbound to reviewing and closing inbound (deals SPC surfaced)
  • Quarry operations run via your existing crew — daily ops are not your job in Florida
  • Your schedule: 3–4 hours of business per day max. Protected family/personal time.
📋

  • SPC hired and 3+ weeks into the job
  • SPC has full HubSpot access and owns pipeline hygiene
  • Call sequence/cadence documented and running
  • Reseller contract status resolved (signed or in negotiation with timeline)
  • 3+ direct B2B accounts with active orders or in proposal stage
  • Weekly SPC call time locked in your Florida calendar
  • Quarry ops manager briefed and autonomous
  • November–April revenue projection built (what does the pipeline suggest will close?)
