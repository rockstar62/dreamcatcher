Foundation
How the Machine Works
Roff-Marsh's core argument: sales fails because salespeople do everything. The fix is division of labor — standardize the workflow, specialize the roles, centralize the scheduling. Every opportunity follows the same path through the same five stages.

"The sales environment is perfectly suited to the application of division of labor. Standardize the workflow. Specialize the resources. Centralize the scheduling. The result is a machine — predictable, scalable, manageable."

— Justin Roff-Marsh, The Machine

The Core Principle

The machine doesn't work halfway. Every stage must exist and every role must be defined — even if one person is playing three roles right now. The value of building it complete today is that when revenue allows you to hire, the seat already exists. You're not creating a job description. You're filling a defined role in a running system.

Stage 1
Origination
Converting suspects into prospects. A suspect is anyone who could theoretically buy. A prospect is someone who has a real reason to buy and has been confirmed as a fit. Origination ends when a prospect agrees to a first conversation.

Stage 2
First Conversation
The BDM's only job in this stage is to listen, qualify, and earn the right to send a sample. This is not a pitch. It is a discovery call — 15 minutes, structured, with a defined outcome.
STAGE 02

The 15-Minute Discovery Call

Goal: Qualify the opportunity and earn permission to send a sample board
Questions to Ask
  • How do you typically source stone for a project?
  • Has a supply or matching issue ever cost you time or a client relationship?
  • What does your client care about when it comes to exterior materials?
  • Do you have anything coming up where natural stone is in the spec?
  • Who else is involved in the material decision?
What You're Listening For
  • Supply pain — late deliveries, mismatched stone
  • Client who cares about provenance or story
  • Active project with stone in scope
  • Decision-maker in the room
  • Openness to switching suppliers
The Mafia Offer — Introduction Point

"We're the only quarry in Michigan producing natural thin and full veneer at volume. We quarry it, process it, and deliver it direct — no distributors in the middle. What that means for you is lot-matching guaranteed start to finish, and a story your client can hold in their hand. We'd like to send you a sample board and spec sheet. If the stone works for your next project, we can talk about what a direct relationship looks like."

Stage Exit Criteria

Prospect has agreed to receive a sample board. Shipping address confirmed. Contact moves to Stage 3 in HubSpot. BDC logs address and triggers sample fulfillment.

Stage 3
Sample
The sample board is the offer made tangible. It goes out within 48 hours of the request. The follow-up sequence begins the day it ships — not when you think they've had time to look at it.

Stage 4
Prosecution
Roff-Marsh defines prosecution as managing the opportunity like a project — scheduling the next activity, moving it forward, never letting it sit idle. Every opportunity in Stage 4 has a defined next action and a date attached to it.
STAGE 04

Quote to Close

Goal: Deliver a quote, handle objections, and get a first order
Prosecution Activities
  • Quote delivered by Connie within 24 hours of request
  • BDM follow-up call 2 days after quote
  • Shop or quarry visit scheduled if still undecided
  • Lot-matching guarantee communicated in writing
  • Objections handled against Layers of Resistance map
Common Objections + Answers
  • "I have a current supplier" → One project trial, no commitment
  • "How do I know it'll match?" → Lot-matching standard document
  • "Lead times?" → Typically 2–3 weeks standard profiles
  • "Never heard of you" → References + quarry visit offer
  • "Price?" → Direct from quarry = no distributor markup
The Shop / Quarry Visit — Highest Conversion Activity

No competitor can replicate a visit to the quarry. A builder or architect who stands at the Stone Mill, meets the team, and hears Steve's story in person does not walk away and spec cultured stone. The quarry visit is the close. It should be offered early in Stage 4 for any undecided prospect.

Stage Exit Criteria

First order placed and deposit received. Contact moves to Stage 5. BDC notifies Ryan (Project Leader) with full order details.

Stage 5
Onboarding
The first order is not the finish line — it is the start of a repeat relationship. Stage 5 is about making the first experience so smooth that the builder or architect never needs to consider another supplier. Ryan owns fulfillment. Steve owns the relationship check-in.
STAGE 05

First Order Through Repeat

Goal: Deliver a flawless first order and set up the next conversation
Project Leader — Ryan's Checklist
  • Confirm lot number and stone profile match sample
  • Production schedule communicated to customer
  • Shipping confirmation with tracking sent
  • Certificate of Origin included with shipment
  • Delivery confirmed by customer
BDM — Steve's Check-In Sequence
  • Call or text day after delivery — "Did it arrive right?"
  • Follow up at install — "How did it go on the wall?"
  • Request a photo of installed stone for portfolio
  • Ask for referral — "Who else do you know building at this level?"
  • Set 90-day reminder for next project conversation

Stage Exit Criteria

Customer has placed a second order or been re-engaged for a future project. Contact is reclassified as an Active Account in HubSpot and moves out of the onboarding pipeline into account management.

People
Roles in the Machine
Roff-Marsh is clear: the machine requires defined roles, not job descriptions that blur. Every person in the machine does one type of work. When one person plays multiple roles, they do so with clear context switching — not simultaneously.

"The BDM performs business-development meetings. That is all. Every other activity — scheduling, data entry, literature fulfillment, proposal generation — is performed by someone else."

— Justin Roff-Marsh, The Machine

Hire Sequence — As Revenue Allows

First hire: BDC. This frees Steve from pipeline management, scheduling, and data entry — giving him more time in the BDM role. This hire multiplies Steve's output without adding another salesperson.

Second hire: Second BDM. Once the machine is proven and pipeline volume exceeds Steve's conversation capacity, a second BDM runs the same workflow — no reinvention required.

Not yet: Do not hire a generalist "salesperson." The machine doesn't have that role. Every hire fills a defined seat in the system.
