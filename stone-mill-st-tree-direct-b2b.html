The Stone Mill, Inc. — S&T Tree #1

The Direct B2B Model

Strategy & Tactics Tree: Why contractors buy direct from the quarry — and what must be true for that to hold at $35M.

What This Tree Is For

S&T Tree Primer
The Purpose of a Strategy & Tactics Tree

An S&T Tree answers one question that most business plans skip entirely: why does this strategy actually work? Not what you're going to do — why the logic of it holds. It maps the chain from Goal → Strategy → Necessary Conditions → Tactics, and at every link it asks: "If we do this, does that actually follow? And what has to be true for it to follow?"

If any link in the chain doesn't hold, the strategy fails — even if every tactic is executed perfectly. The tree finds the weak links before you spend two years finding out the hard way.

What We Now Know About Stone Mill's Model

After the board questions, here is the accurate picture the tree is built from:

  • → ~$1.4M is B2B at $8.50/sq ft — contractors buying direct from Stone Mill. You ARE the distributor in these transactions.
  • → ~$300K is B2C at $13–15/sq ft — homeowners buying direct, bypassing contractors entirely.
  • → Distributors who carry similar stone mark it up above $13 — meaning Stone Mill at $13 direct to contractors is still below what they'd pay a distributor for comparable stone.
  • → The constraint is awareness, not price — contractors who know Stone Mill exists and understand the direct model have a clear financial reason to buy here.
The strategic question is not "why would they pay more." It's "why don't they already know we exist — and what happens when they do?"
The Central Strategic Insight

Stone Mill sits in a position most businesses never achieve: you can give B2B contractors a better price than a distributor AND make more margin than your current wholesale rate simultaneously.

At $8.50 to contractors now → move to $11–12 direct (below distributor, above your current rate) → contractor saves money, Stone Mill earns more. That is a Mafia Offer. Nobody loses except the distributor who isn't in the room.

The S&T Tree maps why that logic holds, what has to be in place for it to work, and where it could break down at scale.

The Strategy & Tactics Tree

Direct B2B Model
The Goal
Grow Stone Mill to $35M revenue / $7M EBITDA
by selling Michigan natural stone direct to B2B contractors
at prices that beat distributors — creating a platform-exit-ready business.
Strategy Layer
S-1
Own the direct channel from quarry to contractor — eliminate the distributor margin layer entirely
Stone Mill is the source AND the seller. No middleman captures the $4–5/sq ft spread.
S-2
Price at $11–13 direct to B2B — below distributor retail, above current $8.50 wholesale
Contractor saves vs. distributor. Stone Mill earns more than current rate. Both win. Distributor loses.
↓
Necessary Conditions — What Must Be True
NC-1
Contractors must know Stone Mill exists and sells direct
Currently the primary gap. Most contractors don't know they can bypass the distributor and buy from a Michigan quarry owner directly.
NC-2
Stone Mill must be able to deliver consistent quality and supply at scale
A contractor won't switch suppliers if they fear running out of matching stone mid-project. Consistency is the non-negotiable.
NC-3
The price advantage must be real and demonstrable — not theoretical
Contractors need to see the math: "You pay X at your distributor. You pay Y here. Same stone. Here's the difference per project."
NC-4
Stone Mill must have a sales system that reaches contractors at scale
Awareness alone doesn't close deals. There must be a process: outreach → sample → conversation → order. Currently running at ~10% capacity.
NC-5
Quarry capacity must grow with revenue — production can't become the ceiling
Current quarries support ~$1.7M. The $35M model requires roughly 20x volume. Capital investment or sourcing expansion is required.
NC-6
No single customer can represent more than 25% of revenue
Platform exit requires diversification. Current 70% concentration is the most urgent structural risk in the entire model.
↓
Tactics — The Specific Actions
T-1 → NC-1 + NC-4
Execute the cold call playbook at 20+ touches/week targeting masons, contractors, and custom builders in Michigan first, then adjacent markets
This is the primary lever right now. Everything else is downstream of this.
T-2 → NC-1 + NC-3
Build a one-page "Direct from Quarry" comparison showing the math — what a 2,000 sq ft project costs through a distributor vs. Stone Mill direct
Make the price advantage tangible and immediate. Leave it with every prospect.
T-3 → NC-2
Implement lot-matching guarantee and documented quality control process — publish it. Make it a named Stone Mill standard.
Consistency fear is the top objection. Remove it structurally, not verbally.
T-4 → NC-4
Hire Sales Process Coordinator Q3 Year 1 — transfer all outbound, scheduling, and HubSpot admin so Steve focuses exclusively on qualified conversations
Steve is currently the bottleneck in the sales system. This hire removes that constraint.
T-5 → NC-5
Get a hard production ceiling number from quarry operations this quarter — then map the capital investment required to reach $7M, $14M, and $35M revenue levels
You cannot commit to revenue targets without knowing where production breaks.
T-6 → NC-6
Set a formal rule: no new deal structure that would push any single customer above 20% of trailing 12-month revenue. Track this monthly in HubSpot.
Diversification doesn't happen accidentally. It requires a deliberate constraint on how you sell.

Logic Test

If → Then → Because
How to Read This

An S&T Tree is only as good as the logic connecting each layer. This page tests every major "if → then" claim in the tree. If the "because" doesn't hold, the link breaks and the strategy fails at that point. Read each one critically — not as a cheerleader but as a skeptic.

If
Stone Mill sells direct to contractors at $11–13/sq ft
Then
Contractors save money vs. buying from a distributor who marks up above $13
Because
Distributors mark up stone above $13 retail. Stone Mill at $11–13 direct is below that ceiling. The savings are real. ✓ This link holds — confirmed by Steve's own pricing context.
If
Contractors save money AND get real Michigan quarry stone
Then
They have a clear reason to switch from their distributor to Stone Mill
Because
Price + provenance + relationship beats distributor convenience — IF the switching cost is low. ⚠ This link is conditional. Switching costs (new account setup, sample approval, habit) are real. The Mafia Offer must make switching easier than staying.
If
Stone Mill runs 20+ outbound prospecting touches per week
Then
Enough contractors learn Stone Mill exists to build a diversified pipeline
Because
Volume of outreach produces awareness at scale. At typical B2B conversion rates (2–5%), 20 touches/week → ~1 new account per month → 12/year. To hit $3.5M Year 1, you need roughly 20+ active accounts. The math works if the system runs. ✓
If
Stone Mill demonstrates lot-to-lot consistency and guarantees it
Then
The primary technical objection to switching ("what if the next order doesn't match?") is removed
Because
Quarrying from owned sources gives Stone Mill more control over consistency than a distributor who aggregates from multiple sources. This is a genuine competitive advantage. ✓ But it must be documented and named — not just claimed verbally.
If
Current quarries support ~$1.7M revenue
Then
Significant capital investment is required before $7M is achievable
Because
4x revenue = roughly 4x volume from same quarry footprint. Equipment, labor, and possibly quarry expansion are required. ⚠ This link is UNKNOWN until Steve gets the production ceiling number from operations. This is a gap in the current plan.
If
Customer concentration drops below 25%
Then
The business qualifies for platform-level acquisition multiples at exit
Because
PE and strategic buyers require no single customer above 20–25% to avoid concentration risk discounts of 1–2x EBITDA. ✓ This link holds — confirmed by M&A research. The 70% current concentration is the single biggest destroyer of exit value.
